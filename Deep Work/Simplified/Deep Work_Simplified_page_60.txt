You have a limited amount of willpower, and it gets used up as you go about your day. Think of willpower like a muscle that gets tired. This is why people in studies had a hard time resisting distractions—they ran out of willpower. The same thing will happen to you, no matter how good your intentions are, unless you manage your habits smartly.

To build a strong work habit, you need to go beyond just wanting to do well. You should create routines and rituals that help you focus without using up too much of your willpower. For example, if you try to switch from browsing the web to doing hard work in the middle of the day, it will take a lot of willpower to change your focus. This often leads to failure. But if you have set times and quiet places for your important tasks, it will be easier to start and keep going. Over time, you’ll find it much easier to do deep work successfully.

The six strategies that follow are like tools to help you create routines and rituals that take into account your limited willpower. They will help you schedule your work and get focused before you start. Some strategies will help motivate you, while others will help recharge your willpower quickly.

You could just decide that deep work is important to you. But using these strategies—or coming up with your own based on the same ideas—will make it much more likely that you can make deep work a big part of your life.
