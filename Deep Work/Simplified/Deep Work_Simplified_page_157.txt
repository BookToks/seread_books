Chapter 1

This chapter talks about Nate Silver and how he brings a lot of visitors to the New York Times website. There’s an article by Tracy and Marc that explains this. It was published on November 6, 2012.

Nate Silver also made a deal with ESPN and ABC News. Mike Allen wrote about it on July 22, 2013.

Some people have raised concerns about how Nate Silver does his work. Sean M. Davis wrote about it on November 1, 2012. Gary Marcus and Ernest Davis also shared their thoughts in an article from January 25, 2013.

David Heinemeier Hansson is mentioned in several places. You can find information about him on his website and in an article by Oliver Lindberg about 37signals. There’s also a Wikipedia page about OAK Racing.

For more details on John Doerr and his business deals, you can check out his Forbes profile. His net worth was reported to be $3.3 billion in April 2014.

The chapter discusses how we are experiencing big changes in technology and the economy. Erik Brynjolfsson and Andrew McAfee wrote about this in their book, highlighting how fast technology is advancing.

They mention that a key question for the future will be whether people can work well with smart machines.

Sherwin Rosen talked about how not every performance is great, and hearing many average singers doesn’t equal one amazing singer.

The chapter also references Jaron Lanier, who discussed Instagram and its impact on job differences.

Lastly, there are tips on how to be successful like Nate Silver, including an article by Walter Hickey that gives nine simple steps.
