Perfect balance might describe empty space in most of the universe. It can also describe many businesses. But every new idea or creation happens when things aren’t balanced.

In real life, outside of economic theories, a business is successful only if it does something no one else can do. So, having a monopoly is not a problem or something rare. It’s actually what every successful business has.

Tolstoy starts Anna Karenina by saying: “All happy families are alike; each unhappy family is unhappy in its own way.” Business is the opposite. All successful companies are different: each one becomes a monopoly by solving a special problem. All failed companies are the same: they couldn’t beat their competitors.
