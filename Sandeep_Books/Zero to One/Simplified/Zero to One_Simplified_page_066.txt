After 10 years, the portfolio won’t be split evenly between winners and losers. Instead, it will mostly have one big winner and a bunch of smaller ones.

But even though this is how it usually ends, it doesn’t feel like that every day.

Investors spend most of their time finding new investments and helping companies that are just starting out. So, most of the companies they deal with are pretty average. The differences they see each day are more about small levels of success, not huge wins or total failures. And because no one wants to give up, investors often spend more time trying to fix struggling companies than working with the most successful ones.
