The NASDAQ, a stock market for tech companies, hit 5,048 in March 2000 but then crashed to 3,321 by April. It kept falling until it reached 1,114 in October 2002. People saw this crash as a kind of punishment for being too hopeful about technology in the ’90s. The time of big dreams was called a time of crazy greed, and many said it was definitely over.

Everyone started to think the future was unclear. People stopped trusting anyone who made big, long-term plans and only cared about short-term results. Instead of technology, people believed globalization would be the future. Since moving from physical stores to online shopping didn’t work out as expected, investors went back to betting on real estate and global markets in countries like Brazil, Russia, India, and China. This caused another bubble, this time in housing.
