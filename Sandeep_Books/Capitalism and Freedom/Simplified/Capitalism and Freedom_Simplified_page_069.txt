Chapter IV: International Financial and Trade Arrangements

The problem with international money systems is how different countries’ currencies relate to each other. For example, how people can exchange U.S. dollars for British pounds or Canadian dollars for U.S. dollars.

This issue is closely linked to how money is controlled, which we talked about in the last chapter. It also connects to government rules about international trade because controlling trade is one way to influence how money moves between countries.

Why International Money Systems Matter for Economic Freedom

Even though international money systems can seem very complicated and technical, people who care about freedom in the economy can’t ignore this topic.
