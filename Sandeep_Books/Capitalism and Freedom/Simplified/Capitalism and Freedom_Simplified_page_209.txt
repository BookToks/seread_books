In the 1920s and 1930s, many smart people in the United States believed that capitalism was a flawed system that stopped people from being truly free and doing well economically. They thought the future would be better if the government had more control over the economy.

These smart people didn’t change their minds because of any real example of a society where the government controlled everything. But the start of communism in Russia and the hope people had in it definitely made them think faster. They compared the unfair and imperfect world they lived in with an ideal, perfect world they imagined.

At that time, there wasn’t much else to compare it to. People had lived through many times when the government controlled the economy closely and got involved in many details.
