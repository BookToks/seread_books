Chapter IX + Occupational licensure

The end of the medieval guild system was an important early step in gaining freedom in the Western world.

It showed that liberal ideas were winning. By the mid-1800s, in Britain, the United States, and to some extent Europe, people could choose any job they wanted without needing permission from the government or any similar authority. 

But in recent years, things have gone backward. More and more, certain jobs can only be done by people who have a license from the state.
