He didn’t come up with the idea himself but took it from a phrase in Mr. Galt’s Annals of the Parish. After using it for a few years, he and others stopped using it because they didn’t like anything that sounded like a label for a certain group or belief.

But as a name for just one idea—not a whole set of ideas—to mean recognizing usefulness as a standard, not a specific way of using it—the term fills a gap in language and often helps avoid long, boring explanations.

An opponent, who is fair and honest (the Rev. J. Llewellyn Davis), disagreed with this and said, "Surely whether saving someone from drowning is right or wrong depends a lot on the reason behind doing it."
