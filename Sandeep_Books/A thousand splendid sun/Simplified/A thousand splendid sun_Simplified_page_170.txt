She had to wonder, lying on the floor with her sweaty hands ready to guide the spoke, if she could ever love Rasheed's child the way she loved Tariq's.

But in the end, Laila couldn't do it.

It wasn’t because she was scared of bleeding to death or because she thought what she was doing was wrong, though she guessed it might be. Laila dropped the spoke because she couldn’t accept what the Mujahideen believed: that sometimes in war, innocent people have to die. Her fight was against Rasheed. The baby was innocent. And there had already been too much killing. Laila had seen too many innocent people get caught and killed in the middle of fights.
