She saw Jalil waving at her, jumping from one stone to another as he crossed the stream, his pockets full of presents. She had hoped so many times that God would give her more time with him. "Thank you," Mariam started to say, but the man on the other end had already hung up.

Rasheed was watching her. Mariam shook her head.

"Useless," he said, grabbing the phone from her. "Like father, like daughter." On their way out of the lobby, Rasheed quickly walked to the coffee table, which was now empty, and took the last ring of jelabi. He took it home and gave it to Zalmai.
