The air between them smelled like tobacco and the onions and grilled lamb they had eaten earlier. Sometimes, his ear brushed against her cheek, and she could feel that he had shaved because it felt scratchy.

When it was over, he rolled off her, breathing hard. He put his forearm over his forehead. In the dark, she could see the blue hands of his watch. They lay there for a while, on their backs, not looking at each other.

"There is nothing embarrassing about this, Mariam," he said, his words a little slurred. "This is what married people do.

Even the Prophet and his wives did this. There is nothing embarrassing." A little later, he pushed the blanket aside and left the room, leaving her with the feeling of his head on her pillow, making her wait through the pain inside her, looking up at the frozen stars in the sky and a cloud covering the moon like a wedding veil.
