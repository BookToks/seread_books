LESSONS LEARNED

They say the party was over in the year 2000, and it was time to wake up! So tonight, I’m going to celebrate like it’s 1999!

The NASDAQ, which is a stock market, hit a high of 5,048 in March 2000 but then dropped to 3,321 by April. It kept falling until it hit a low of 1,114 in October 2002. By then, people thought the market crash was a sign that the excitement about technology in the ’90s was over. What was once seen as a time of hope turned into a time of greed, and everyone agreed that it was definitely finished.

People started to see the future as uncertain and thought anyone with big plans that took years to develop was just being unrealistic. Instead of focusing on technology, everyone turned to globalization as the new hope. Since the shift from traditional businesses to online ones didn’t work out as expected, investors went back to real estate and global markets. This led to another bubble, but this time in housing.
