The euro started in January 1999, but a lot of people were unsure about it. On its first day, it was worth $1.19, but then it dropped to $0.83 in just two years. By mid-2000, the G7 central bankers had to step in and spend billions to support it.

At the same time, there was a short-lived dot-com boom that began in September 1998. The world was struggling, and the old ways of doing business weren't working well with globalization. Something had to change and be really successful if the future was going to improve. The internet and the New Economy seemed to be the only way to move forward.
