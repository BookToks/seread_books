AMERICAN ROYALTY

Celebrities are often seen as “American royalty.” We even give special titles to our favorite stars: 

Elvis Presley was called the king of rock. 

Michael Jackson was known as the king of pop. 

Britney Spears was the pop princess. 

But then things changed. Elvis had a rough time in the ’70s and died alone in a bad situation. Now, his impersonators don’t look cool like he did. Michael Jackson went from being a loved child star to someone who struggled with drugs and had a lot of problems. People were really interested in his troubles. Britney’s story is the craziest of all. We built her up from nothing and made her a huge star when she was just a teenager. But then everything went wrong: she shaved her head, had issues with eating, and faced a big court case over her kids. Was she always a little off? Did the fame mess with her mind? Or did she act out to get more attention?

For some stars who fell from grace, death seems to bring them back to life in a way. Many famous musicians have died at age 27—like Janis Joplin, Jimi Hendrix, Jim Morrison, and Kurt Cobain.
